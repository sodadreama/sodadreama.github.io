Progestogens

This class of hormones has a somewhat nebulous intended usage in feminizing hormone therapy. There are many anecdotal usages, e.g. breast enlargement or additional feminization, but none in transgender women that have been studied systematically.

There are various chemicals under the name of progestogens, however what I'll be talking about in this article is progesterone; this hormone can strongly inhibit sex-hormone production, which is also why some people use them in feminizing hormone therapy. This may also be used, topically, in treating androgen-dependent conditions of the skin and hair, like oily skin or scalp hair loss. This is because progesterone can actually reduce levels of dihydrotestosterone (DHT) locally when applied topically.
